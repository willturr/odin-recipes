Carbonara

Description

This is a recipe for a lovely simple carbonara that is adapted from Jamie Oliver. Jamie seems to think that adding double cream is a good idea, but really it adds nothing of substance to the recipe.

He also suggests that it should be served with penne, whereas I prefer a more traditional spaghetti.

Ingredients (serves 4-5)

  • 500g Spaghetti
  • 200g Parmeganno Regianno
  • A Pack of Panchetta/A few sliced bacon rashers
  • 5 Egg yolks
  • Plenty of salt and pepper to season

Steps

  1. Place pasta in to boil; the sauce can be prepped while it is cooking.
  2. Grate all the parmesan, why not.
  3. Separate the yolks from the eggs and place into a bowl, mixing in the cheese
  4. Season generously with pepper, sparingly with salt.
  5. Fry the panchetta/bacon till browning, allowing to cool a bit before incorporating into sauce
  6. Drain pasta once cooked, allowing a few seconds for slight cooling
  7. Incorporate the pasta and the sauce, keeping the mixture moving so eggs don't cook
  8. Serve once sauce is fully coating all the pasta, shining in beautiful cheesy radiance
  9. Serve to those you love.
